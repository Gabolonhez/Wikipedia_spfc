São Paulo Futebol Clube

São Paulo FC, nicknamed "Tricolor" for its red, white, and black colors, is a highly decorated Brazilian soccer club. They are considered one of the most successful in Brazil, and even hold the title of "Champions of Everything" for having won every currently available title.

Foundation

São Paulo FC's story begins in 1930, born from the union of two historic clubs – Club Athletico Paulistano and Associação Atlética das Palmeiras. This merger marked the rise of a new powerhouse, overcoming early challenges to establish itself as a force to be reckoned with in Brazilian football.

Faithful

The heart of São Paulo FC beats with its passionate fanbase, the Tricolor Faithful. Renowned for their unwavering support and devotion, these millions of fans (over 22 million strong!) clad in red, white, and black create an unforgettable atmosphere that fuels the team's drive for victory.

Stadium

More than just a stadium, the Morumbi is a hallowed ground for São Paulo FC. This iconic 72,000-seat arena, nicknamed "The Fortress," pulsates with the energy of the Tricolor Faithful. The stadium's rich history and electrifying atmosphere solidify its place as a temple of South American football.
